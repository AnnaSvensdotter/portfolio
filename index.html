Anna Svensdotter

About

Anna works for the Australian Institute of Architects as a Membership Specialist (Advocacy and Research). She is also a Lecturer, Research Assistant and Sessional Academic with the Centre for Justice, QUT School of Law, Urban Informatics Research Group, QUT Design Lab and School of Design at the Queensland University of Technology in Brisbane, Australia. She graduated B.Des Architectural Studies (1st Class Honours) at QUT 2013, M.Arch QUT 2014. Anna also coordinates courses in design and architecture at the University of Queensland and the Sunshine Coast University.

Anna completed her PhD Border Nomads: An Architectural Investigation of Transient Public Urban Place. She is a transdisciplinary researcher with a core focus on spatialjustice. Anna teach across all disciplines of design at QUT, UQ, and USC in areas such as visualisation, design studio (Architecture and Landscape Architecture), design theory, design history at undergraduate and postgraduate levels. Anna’s PhD explores the significance and meaning of transient public urban place to urban actors who operate in the societal margin.

She has been involved with several research projects eg The InstaBooth, HCI After Dark, Dignity First Homelessness Hackathon, The Hospital Burrow Project and the QUT Art Museum exhibition Vis-Ability (Tactile Model) since graduating from the Master of Architecture at the School of Design at QUT at the end of 2014. Anna’s research is transdisciplinary with a focus on socio-spatial structures of the urban public space, spatial justice, urban assemblages and social affordances of the public place, temporal civic activity, territoriality, and temporary public urban places. Driven by a curiosity of the authentically imperfect and temporal found in a transient urban public place. Anna is supervised by Dr Mirko Guaralda, Dr Severine Mayere, Dr Janice Rieger and Jaz Hee-Jong Choi (external).
